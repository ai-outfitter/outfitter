Post-quantum (quantum-resistant) encryption replaces RSA and elliptic-curve schemes, which Shor’s algorithm breaks on a large quantum computer, with problems believed hard even for quantum machines: NIST has standardized the lattice-based ML-KEM (Kyber) for key exchange and ML-DSA (Dilithium) plus the hash-based SLH-DSA for signatures, and the practical work now is hybrid deployments and migrating long-lived secrets before "harvest now, decrypt later" attacks pay off.
